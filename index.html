WHY IT MATTERS

Attackers don't break in. They get let in.

✉️

Email is the front door

Phishing, spear phishing and BEC remain the cheapest, highest-yield way to get a foothold — no exploit required, just a convincing sender and a sense of urgency.

🏢

Buildings have people, not just badges

Tailgating, piggybacking and impersonation exploit politeness and hierarchy — the instinct to hold a door or trust a lanyard.

📱

The attack surface fits in a pocket

Smishing, vishing and QR-code lures move the con onto the device you trust most and check most often.

🧠

Awareness is a trained reflex

Like any skill, spotting manipulation improves with repetition and feedback — which is exactly what a simulator can safely provide.
